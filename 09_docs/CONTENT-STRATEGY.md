# Content Strategy — The Two-Tier System

The single most important idea in this template: your online presence should be **two
complementary sites**, not one overloaded page. This doc tells you — and your agents —
what to put where.

## The two tiers

| | **Online CV** | **Personal Hub** |
|---|---|---|
| Role | Professional front door | Content home |
| Audience | Recruiters, collaborators, hiring managers | Anyone — peers, students, the public |
| Bar | "Does this show how I think at a high level?" | "Is this useful or interesting?" |
| Language | Primary language, curated | Bilingual, broad |
| Cadence | Few pieces, high standard | Frequent, casual |
| Examples | Publications, technical perspectives | Tutorials, career notes, life posts |

The two sites are **complementary, not duplicative**. Neither republishes the other.
The Online CV is the curated professional layer; the Personal Hub is the broad, long-horizon
content base.

## Content routing decision

For any new piece of writing, route it like this:

```
   Is it in your second language?
        └── yes ──►  Personal Hub  (second-language series)

   Is it English but casual / broad / tutorial-style?
        └── yes ──►  Personal Hub  (general series)

   Is it technical and something you'd hand a hiring manager?
        └── yes ──►  Online CV  →  "Perspectives"

   Is it a paper or formal research output?
        └── yes ──►  Online CV  →  "Publications"
```

## Why separate them

- **Different bars.** The CV's "Perspectives" should make a reader at a top lab think
  *"this person has unusual clarity about X."* A how-to post doesn't belong there — it
  dilutes the signal. The Hub welcomes it.
- **Different SEO targets.** The CV optimizes for *your name + field*. The Hub optimizes
  for the *topics* people search.
- **Different maintenance rhythms.** The Hub can go quiet without hurting you; the CV
  should never look abandoned (no dead placeholder tabs — remove a tab until it has content).

## Cadence

- **Online CV / Perspectives:** at least one piece per quarter. Trigger = a real milestone
  or a position worth defending publicly, not a calendar slot.
- **Personal Hub:** as often as you have something useful to say.

> Placeholder links signal abandonment; absent tabs signal intentionality.
> When in doubt, ship fewer, better pieces on the CV and route the rest to the Hub.

## How the agents use this

- **content-strategist** consults this file before drafting or filing any content.
- **growth-marketer** uses the tier to choose the right SEO target and the right
  distribution channel (e.g. a LinkedIn post linking back to a new Perspectives piece).
- **site-engineer** files the generated page into the correct tier's folder.
